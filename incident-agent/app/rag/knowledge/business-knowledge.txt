INVOICE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM — BUSINESS OVERVIEW
==============================================

WHAT THE SYSTEM DOES
--------------------
The Invoice Management System allows businesses to submit vendor invoices in bulk
by uploading a CSV file. The system validates each invoice, processes it, and tracks
whether it was accepted or rejected. At the end of a batch, earnings can be calculated
per vendor to determine their total payout including applicable bonuses.


WHO USES IT
-----------
- Operations / Finance teams who upload invoice files on behalf of the business
- Management who review batch results and vendor earnings
- Support teams who investigate why certain invoices were rejected


KEY CONCEPTS
------------

Vendor
  A supplier or service provider registered in the system.
  Every invoice must reference a known vendor. If the vendor does not exist,
  the invoice is rejected. Vendors are identified by a short code (e.g. "ACME")
  and a full name (e.g. "Acme Corp").

Invoice
  A single invoice submitted by a vendor. Each invoice has:
  - A unique invoice ID (assigned by the submitter)
  - A vendor it belongs to
  - An amount and currency
  - An issue date (the date the invoice was originally issued)
  - A status: Pending, Processed, or Failed

Batch Upload
  When a CSV file is uploaded, all invoices in it form a batch.
  Each batch gets a unique ID. The batch tracks how many invoices
  were accepted and how many were rejected.

Earnings Calculation
  After a batch is processed, the finance team can calculate vendor earnings.
  The system sums up all successfully processed invoice amounts per vendor
  and applies a bonus based on the total:
  - Below 2,000 → no bonus
  - Between 2,000 and 4,000 → 10% bonus
  - Above 4,000 → 20% bonus
  The result is the final payout amount per vendor for that batch.


TYPICAL WORKFLOW
----------------
1. Finance team prepares a CSV file with invoices from one or more vendors.
2. The file is uploaded through the system.
3. Each invoice is validated and processed. Some may fail due to data issues.
4. The team reviews which invoices passed and which failed.
5. Once processing is complete, earnings are calculated for the batch.
6. The finance team uses the earnings report to determine vendor payouts.


REASONS AN INVOICE CAN BE REJECTED
------------------------------------
- The vendor code in the file does not match any registered vendor
- The invoice ID already exists in the system (duplicate submission)
- The invoice issue date is more than 2 years old
- The CSV file contains formatting errors (missing fields, invalid currency format, wrong date format)


WHAT CUSTOMERS MIGHT REPORT AS PROBLEMS
-----------------------------------------
- "My invoice was not processed" → likely a vendor mismatch, duplicate ID, or date issue
- "The whole upload failed" → likely a formatting problem in the CSV file
- "The earnings are wrong or missing" → invoices may still be processing, or some failed
- "A vendor is missing from the earnings report" → that vendor had no successfully processed invoices in the batch
